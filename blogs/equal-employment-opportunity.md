People of today make conscious decisions- be it life or work. In life, they safeguard their own interests. At work, the “Equal Employment Opportunity” act protects it for them.

With the evolving world, the nature of work-life has changed as well. But there is one major business change that we have seen. That change is known as Equal Employment Opportunity or EEO.  This has enabled workplaces to [empower the employees](/en/blog/employee-empowerment/) and promote a diverse workforce.

EEO has set some golden standards. In return, it significantly improves and enhances organizational performance in the long run. With EEO, skilled people are given equal chances to showcase their talents. Meanwhile, companies benefit from the new ideas and creativity that they bring to the table.

But what is it exactly? This article will discuss the definition and the advantages of practicing EEO.

## What is Equal Employment Opportunity?

> “All of us do not have equal talent, but all of us should have an equal opportunity to develop our talent.” -John F. Kennedy

Equal employment opportunity refers to the concept of treating all equally in terms of employment. There is no discrimination based on race, gender, religion, ethnicity, color, age, disability, or other factors.

To reduce the chances of discrimination, many countries have introduced specific laws. Organizations have to follow these laws and adhere to them strictly.

Some cases of EEO are the [Employment Act of 1967](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/a/age-discrimination-employment-act-1967.asp) for the US or [Articles 15 and 16 for India](https://www.upr-info.org/sites/default/files/document/india/session_13_-_may_2012/a_hrc_wg.6_13_ind_1_india_annexii.pdf). Many countries have their own version of EEO. But the aim remains the same. To promote equal opportunities for everyone.

There are federal agencies that ensure the implementation of EEO acts and rules. The work of these agencies is to-

- Prohibit discrimination
- Promote gender equality
- Create awareness about LGBTQIA+ rights
- Check whether the organizations are following federal laws
- Look after any complaint and solve them swiftly

Without EEO, employees and managers are more inclined towards burnout. They feel the risk of missing out on growth opportunities. This hampers their morale and ability to work with efficiency.

## The Need for Equal Employment Opportunity

> “We live in a moment when the dream of equal opportunity is within reach.” -Barack Obama

With the rise in globalization and digitization, the need for a capable workforce is critical. Therefore, you need to make sure that your organization excels in every aspect.

And to do that, the need for an equal employment opportunity is vital. When you opt for such practices, it opens up a whole section of skilled people waiting to be hired.

But sometimes, the numbers tell a different story.

- [According to a study](https://www.utmsjoe.mk/files/Vol.%206%20No.%202/UTMSJOE-2015-0602-009-Stoilkovska-Ilieva-Gjakovski.pdf), 47% of applicants have felt that nationality plays a significant role in the selection process.

![47-](https://blogimage.vantagecircle.com/content/images/2021/05/47-.png)

- Moreover, [the study also found that 52%](https://www.utmsjoe.mk/files/Vol.%206%20No.%202/UTMSJOE-2015-0602-009-Stoilkovska-Ilieva-Gjakovski.pdf) of the respondents have been discriminated against when it came to political affiliation.

![52-](https://blogimage.vantagecircle.com/content/images/2021/05/52-.png)

[Another survey states that](https://www.aarp.org/work/working-at-50-plus/info-2018/age-discrimination-common-at-work.html) about 3 in 5 older workers have experienced age discrimination in the workplace.

These stats make it even more critical for the organizations to facilitate a system that would encourage EEO. And end discrimination for the greater good of the corporate world.

## Summing it Up!

Equal employment is everyone’s right. That is something one cannot take away from people. Organizations need to acknowledge this fact and practice fair opportunities for everyone. Creating awareness is vital, and leaders need to adopt the idea of EEO.

It might take time. But at the end of the day, the focus should be on creating a positive environment for the employees. When an organization successfully creates the desired workplace, it elevates productivity. Moreover, employees become efficient and confident in their work. Also, teams collaborate well to accomplish the long-term goals of the organization.
