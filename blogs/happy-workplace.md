There is a [31% rise in productivity](https://hbr.org/2011/06/the-happiness-dividend) when your organization has happy employees.

As an employer, it is hard to oversee such an important statistic when productivity comes into play.

Having happy employees in the organization is only possible when you create a happy workplace. A working environment where every employee looks forward to coming back to.

Big names in the corporate world have learned that fostering a fun and [learning environment](/en/blog/continuous-learning/) has always led them to success. They have unlocked the key to [employee happiness](/en/blog/employee-happiness-workplace/). Happy employees are loyal, satisfied and show dedication in what they do. When employees are happy, it has a direct impact on your bottom line.

I know it is easier said than done.

Creating a happy workplace requires a reasonable amount of time and good strategy. This article will provide a quick guide on tips for building a happy workplace.

## 6 Tips to a Happy Workplace

### 1. Recognition on the Loop

A [Gallup survey](https://www.gallup.com/workplace/236441/employee-recognition-low-cost-high-impact.aspx) has revealed that employees will quit the organization if they are not recognized. Thus, increasing the rates of employee turnover.

A situation every organization tries to avoid because they can cost a fortune.

With the [practice of recognition](/en/blog/giving-recognition-at-work/), avoiding such circumstances is easier. And with the rise in workplace stress, it has become vital that you provide the necessary recognition. When you acknowledge your employee’s efforts-

- They feel valued
- It generates a sense in them that they are doing the good work.

This will not only help you to keep them happy and satisfied, but it also builds loyalty in your employees.

### 2. Meaningful Work

To become proficient in their work, the employees need to understand their expectations. They need to go through the entire structure of the workflow and resort to what suits them best.

When your employees find meaning in their work, they are more likely to excel in what they do. And to do this, you must delegate them with the right task.

It is important that the employees maintain their interest in the job. Employees should connect to their work that will make them enthusiastic and [engaged](/en/blog/engaged-employees/).

### 3. Rest Places at Workplace

Let us admit, we have all felt exhaustion at one point during work hours. That can pose a threat to productivity and engagement. When employees feel exhausted at work, they are more prone to not invest their time in their jobs.

An excellent solution to that will be providing rest hours for the employees. You can provide a dedicated space for your employees to relax for an hour and rejuvenate their energy for the rest of the day.

Investing in plans like these will not only improve your [company culture](/en/blog/company-culture/), but it will also make you stand out from your competitors. And there are higher chances that your employees will be happy and satisfied with their working environment. Hence, increasing [employee engagement](/en/blog/employee-engagement/) and fostering a happy workplace.

### 4. Emphasizing on Psychological Safety

[Psychological safety](/en/blog/psychological-safety/) is a shared belief that the employees and team members are open about admitting mistakes, experimenting with new things, and speaking up without criticism.

When you come to think of an environment like that, it proves to be quite crucial. A workplace where psychological safety exists [becomes more transparent](/en/blog/transparency-in-the-workplace/), and employees feel more connected with their work. They are open to trying out new ways to complete their tasks and not refrain from discussing them out with their peers.

A sense of autonomy increases, dependency on others decreases, and the employees become more innovative.

Such environments lead to a happy workplace because employees can push themselves to unknown limits. There is always learning in that process, and employees new things in the long run.

### 5. Game Time

*“All work and no play makes Jack a dull boy”*, we all know how true this proverb is. Piling up an employee’s desk with too much work will create tremendous pressure. Leading to an increased rate of [stress in the workplace](https://blog.vantagefit.io/workplace-stress/) and a rise in [burnout levels](/en/blog/employee-burnout/).

But, you can change that by reducing work and providing them some time for an active gaming session. Giving the employees a much-needed break between work is quite essential. Not only will it reduce their stress, but it will also help them become  fit.

With gaming sessions on the cards, you will likely provide them an environment where they can bond well with other employees. This, in turn, will make them feel more purposeful at their workplace and increase productivity.

Leading to [job satisfaction](/en/blog/job-satisfaction/) and increasing happiness at work.

### 6. Work-Life Balance

The quality of life is changing with time. Along with that, it has become necessary to maintain a good work-life balance. It is a driving factor when it comes to job satisfaction.

If you, as a manager, want to get a happier workplace, you must encourage people to maintain an excellent [work-life balance](/en/blog/work-life-balance/). How employees feel about the organization all depends on how much you support them in the long run.

Provide them the opportunity to [work from home](/en/blog/work-from-home-policy/), including flexibility in the schedule and much more.

Doing positive things for your employees which help them to maintain their work-life balance will bear positive results for you. Your employees will be more loyal, enthusiastic, dedicated, and push themselves to achieve the organization's goals.

## Summing it up

Organizations need to understand that keeping their employees happy is one of the most critical tasks that they need to focus on. Achieving a happy workplace can be an extensive task, but in the long run, it has positive results. A happy workplace means happy employees. And with your employees pleased within the organization, they are more likely to be [morally boosted](/en/blog/employee-morale-boosters/), [loyal](/en/blog/employee-loyalty/), and motivated.

So, invest in your plans to create a healthy and happy workplace that will make you stand out from your competitors.
